# Memo Structure Reference

Use this when building or reordering a memo.

## Syndicate / LP Memo Structure

Best default:

1. **Overview:** one to three paragraphs plus a deal table.
2. **Investment history:** if this is a follow-on, show original investment, ownership basis, and what changed.
3. **Our investment thesis:** 5-7 strong bullets with bold lead-in, colon, then concise explanation.
4. **Comparable companies and transactions:** table with valuation context, revenue/run-rate context, and read-through.
5. **Traction / revenue performance:** chart or compact table, with source/basis footnote.
6. **Market opportunity:** explain demand shift in plain language; no generic TAM padding.
7. **Business lines / product vectors:** compact table or bullets; avoid lengthy product manual if audience knows the company.
8. **Team:** founder and key hires; interpret what team composition signals.
9. **Risks and challenges:** practical watch-items, not a full internal diligence theory.
10. **Conclusion:** one decisive paragraph.
11. **Round details:** terms, allocation, minimum, rights, carry, SPV fees, legal entity, website, disclaimers.

## Internal / IC Memo Structure

Best default:

1. Recommendation.
2. Investment view and decision math.
3. What changed since last round or first investment.
4. Key facts.
5. Thesis.
6. Product and moat.
7. Sector coverage and comps.
8. Signal ledger / diligence evidence.
9. Risks, bear case, and diligence gates.
10. 14-day diligence plan.
11. Final view.
12. Sources or appendix.

## Portfolio Follow-On Emphasis

For a follow-on, the memo should not read like a cold deal memo. Make the frame:

- We know the company.
- The company has materially changed.
- The current round is not priced like the original entry.
- The question is whether new scale, customer quality, margins, and ownership rights justify additional exposure.

Important sections:

- Original check, date, cap/valuation, and current ownership basis.
- "What changed since we invested."
- "Why the round is still investable at this price."
- Current ownership/pro-rata mechanics, if relevant and approved.

## Thesis Bullet Pattern

Use this structure:

**Point label:** Explanation in one compact paragraph. Lead with the investment judgment, then support with facts.

Good labels:

- Frontier AI demand
- Demand from the right buyers
- Exceptional growth with profitability
- Coherent product expansion
- Differentiated access
- Price discipline
- Exit potential

Avoid labels like "Huge TAM" or "AI tailwinds."

## Exit Potential Pattern

For strategic outcomes, use buy-vs-build correctly: a buyer may prefer to acquire a capability rather than build it internally.

Example:

**Exit potential:** As a buy-vs-build target, the company could be attractive to buyers that may prefer to acquire scaled infrastructure rather than recreate it internally. Alternatively, if the company remains neutral across major customers, an IPO may be the cleaner path as a standalone infrastructure platform.

Do not imply a current customer is likely to acquire the company unless that is sourced or approved.
