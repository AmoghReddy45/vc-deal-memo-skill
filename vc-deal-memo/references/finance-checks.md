# Finance and Underwriting Checks

Use this when working with numbers, comps, round details, and diligence logic.

## ARR vs Run-Rate vs Revenue

Never conflate these:

- **ARR:** recurring contractual revenue, usually subscription or committed recurring spend.
- **Annualized run-rate:** current month or period multiplied by 12. Can include project work, SOWs, pilots, or lumpy usage.
- **Recognized revenue:** accounting revenue over a period.
- **Gross billings / GMV:** total customer spend before pass-through costs.
- **Net revenue:** revenue retained after pass-throughs, expert pay, marketplace take-rate, or reimbursables, depending on business model.

If uncertain:

> Treat public ARR language as reported run-rate until contract structure proves recurring revenue.

## Valuation and Price Discipline

Do not compare a past round price to future revenue as if it were the current underwriting price. For follow-ons, the relevant question is current round price and expected exit math.

Useful framing:

- Current valuation / current gross run-rate.
- Current valuation / current or next-year recognized revenue.
- Current valuation / estimated net revenue, if gross vs net matters.
- Exit hurdle for 3x or 5x returns after assumed dilution.

Example:

> At [valuation], this is no longer early-entry pricing; the case is that current scale, customer quality, and profitability justify paying up for additional exposure.

## Ownership and Pro-Rata

For follow-ons, state basis carefully:

- current fully diluted basis,
- post-SAFE or post-round basis,
- whether unconverted SAFEs are included,
- whether rights are contractual or assumed,
- whether allocation is direct fund, syndicate/SPV, or both.

Avoid telling the firm to verify its own cap table. Use:

> Actual sizing depends on internal cap-table position, available pro-rata rights, reserve capacity, and conversion mechanics.

## Comps Table Standards

Comps should be directionally useful, not fake precision.

Columns:

- Company / transaction
- Valuation context
- Revenue / run-rate context
- Read-through for target

Always note if:

- a transaction has strategic premium,
- a valuation is reported/rumored,
- revenue is gross run-rate rather than recognized revenue,
- a business model differs materially,
- the comp is a warning case rather than upside case.

## Diligence Gates

For internal memos, use specific thresholds when possible:

- Top customer concentration.
- Top 3 or top 10 customer share.
- Gross vs net revenue.
- Gross margin and margin expansion path.
- Net retention / repeat usage.
- Customer reference quality.
- Product-line revenue mix.
- Cash balance, burn, runway, and profitability.

For LP memos, convert these to "watch-items" unless the memo is meant to be heavily diligence-forward.
